## Head-to-head table

Puerto Vallarta wins walkability and often net yield with Zona Romántica delivering 4–5% returns at $280K–420K entry, while Los Cabos offers desert luxury branding and Baja scarcity premium at $350K+ with 3–4% net, creating Pacific coast choice between operational efficiency versus premium positioning.

| Factor | Los Cabos | Puerto Vallarta |
|---|---|---|
| States | Baja California Sur | Jalisco (+ Nayarit bay) |
| Airports | SJD | PVR |
| 1BR entry | $350K+ | $280K–450K |
| Net yield (prime) | 3–4.5% | 3.5–5% |
| Walkable STR core | San José centro | Zona Romántica |
| Retiree ecosystem | Moderate | Strong |
| Character | Desert + luxury | Mountain bay + old town |
| HOA typical | $400–1,200 | $200–600 |
| Owner-use bias | High | Balanced |
| First-time Pacific buyer | San José path | Romántica path |

## Entry price and budget tiers

Puerto Vallarta offers accessible entry near $280K in Versalles zones while Los Cabos maintains $350K+ floors in San José centro, reflecting PV's broader price spectrum versus Cabos premium positioning, though both markets add 5–10% closing costs on all tiers.

| Tier | Los Cabos | Puerto Vallarta |
|---|---:|---:|
| Value 1BR | Limited ~$350K floor | ~$280K Versalles |
| Core investor 1BR | $350K–500K SJC | $300K–420K Romántica |

| Closing | 5–10% both | 5–10% both |

## Los Cabos advantages

Los Cabos delivers desert-coastal luxury branding with Baja scarcity and mature California second-home culture, while San José walkability within Cabos offers arts district authenticity that competes with PV on pedestrian experience despite higher entry pricing and premium market positioning.

Desert-coastal luxury brand: Global recognition distinct from bay-and-mountains PV.

Baja scarcity: Transpeninsular geography limits sprawl versus Banderas Bay expansion.

California second-home culture: Mature owner-user base renting selectively.

San José walkability: Arts district competes with PV on evening stroll thesis within Cabos.

Sport fishing / marina (CSL): Event-driven ADR niches PV does not replicate.

## Puerto Vallarta advantages

Puerto Vallarta's Zona Romántica provides walkable STR operations with established retiree infrastructure and net yield advantages, often beating Cabo Corridor performance at lower price points while offering culinary tourism depth and Nuevo Vallarta resort-family alternatives on shared PVR airport access.

Walkable STR core: Zona Romántica cobblestones — guest reviews cite dining walk without car.

Retiree infrastructure: Healthcare, expat services, longer-stay owner ecosystem.

Net yield edge: Romántica often beats Cabo Corridor on net at lower price point.

Culinary tourism depth: Restaurant density supports shoulder-season ADR.

Nuevo Vallarta option: Resort-family thesis on same PVR airport without leaving bay market.

## Walkability and guest experience

Puerto Vallarta's full Zona Romántica walkability outperforms Los Cabos where car dependency affects most zones except San José centro, creating guest logistics advantages for PV STR operators who prioritize review scores and reduced transportation friction over desert luxury branding.

| Element | Los Cabos | Puerto Vallarta |
|---|---|---|
| Romántica-style old town | San José partial | Full Zona Romántica |
| Car required | Often yes | Romántica no |
| Hillside stairs | Some Pedregal | Conchas Chinas |
| Marina tourism | CSL + Corridor | Marina + Nuevo Vallarta |
| Nightlife STR reviews | CSL party risk | Romántica mixed |

STR operators prioritising guest walk scores default PV Romántica unless targeting Cabos luxury ADR.

## Retiree vs investor thesis

Puerto Vallarta absorbs retiree inflows — buildings may restrict STR as owner mix shifts. Verify regime de condominio.

Los Cabos skews affluent second-home — fewer retiree-budget towers but higher HOA luxury stacks.

Retiree not renting: PV often wins. Pure STR: Romántica vs San José — tie on net, PV cheaper entry.

## Flight connectivity

SJD and PVR airports serve overlapping US west-coast and Texas markets with seasonal route variations, requiring origin-specific research since both hubs compete for Southern California and Bay Area traffic while Midwest coverage varies annually between destinations.

| Origin bias | Cabos (SJD) | PV (PVR) |
|---|---|---|
| Southern California | Strong | Strong |
| Bay Area | Strong | Strong |
| Pacific Northwest | Moderate | Moderate |
| Texas | Strong | Strong |
| Midwest | Seasonal | Seasonal |
| Canada | Winter seasonal | Winter seasonal |

Check your city's direct route map before assuming either hub.

## Risks compared

Los Cabos faces high HOA escalation in Corridor zones and desert water stress while Puerto Vallarta encounters retiree HOA politics affecting STR allowances and hillside utility pressure, creating different execution risks where Cabos emphasizes cost management and PV requires HOA relationship maintenance.

| Risk | Los Cabos | Puerto Vallarta |
|---|---|---|
| HOA escalation | High Corridor | Moderate |
| Water / utilities | Desert stress | Hillside pressure |
| STR HOA bans | Yes | Retiree politics |
| Summer occupancy | Shoulder soft | Shoulder soft |
| State admin | BCS unified | Jalisco vs Nayarit |
| Ejido bargains | Inland Baja | Bay fringe |
