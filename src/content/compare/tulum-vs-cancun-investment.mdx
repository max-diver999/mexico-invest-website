## Cancún investment profile

Cancún offers Mexico's busiest international airport hub with decades of foreign-buyer precedent in hotel zone condos, providing institutional stability and mature property management markets that reduce operational complexity compared to frontier Riviera Maya markets.

### Strengths

- CUN airport — one of Mexico's busiest; constant inbound guests
- Convention and business travel layer beyond leisure
- Decades of foreign-buyer legal precedent in hotel zone
- Puerto Cancún and Zona Hotelera resale depth
- Mature property management market

### Weaknesses

Hotel zone premium pricing and HOA costs of $400–800+ monthly create net yield compression, while established all-inclusive hotel competition caps ADR during shoulder periods and limits STR pricing power in mature tourism corridors.

- Premium pricing in prime zones
- HOA can run $400–800+ in luxury towers
- Hotel-zone STR regulation competitive and saturated in spots
- Less "walkable small town" than Playa Centro

Area detail: [Cancún](/areas/cancun/).

## Tulum investment profile

Tulum leverages global wellness branding for premium ADR positioning in eco-luxury segments while offering lower entry tickets in inland zones, though Region 15 oversupply creates significant net yield dispersion and resale liquidity challenges for undifferentiated product.

### Strengths

- Global wellness and eco brand commands ADR premium in niches
- Lower entry ticket in inland zones
- Felipe Carrillo Puerto airport long-term access tailwind
- Aldea Zama master plan with paved infrastructure
- Lifestyle buyer demand for new product

### Weaknesses

Region 15 tower oversupply drives net yields below 3% in many buildings while car dependency hurts STR convenience scores, creating operational friction and permit enforcement variability that increases execution risk for remote foreign owners compared to established Cancún systems.

- Region 15 tower glut — net yields under 3% common
- Car dependency hurts STR convenience scores in many zones
- Permit enforcement variability
- Ejido proximity on marketed "cheap" land
- Thinner resale if thesis fails vs Cancún hotel zone

## Yield comparison (indicative 1BR)

Cancún Zona Hotelera delivers 3.5–4.2% net yields while Tulum spans 2.4–4% net depending on zone, with Puerto Cancún and Aldea Zama showing moderate performance while Region 15 underperforms both markets due to oversupply and HOA burden on identical units.

| Zone | Gross | Net |
|---|---:|---:|
| Cancún Zona Hotelera | 5.5–6.5% | 3.5–4.2% |
| Puerto Cancún | 5–6% | 3.5–4% |
| Tulum Aldea Zama | 6–7% | 3.2–4% |
| Tulum Region 15 | 5.5–6% | 2.4–3% |

## Who should choose Tulum

- Lifestyle investor accepting lower net for brand exposure
- Value hunter who will walk from Region 15 oversupply

## Bottom line

Neither city wins unconditionally. Cancún is the infrastructure and liquidity play. Tulum is the branding and selective-value play with higher execution risk. Most STR-first investors still anchor in Playa del Carmen between them — then add Tulum or Cancún exposure once they have operational experience and verified local counsel.

Run the same net yield spreadsheet on both cities with identical occupancy and HOA assumptions — the spreadsheet usually tells you which city fits before the lifestyle marketing does.

When in doubt between these two, visit both for three nights each as a guest — operational friction you feel as a tourist often predicts STR review scores.

Add Playa del Carmen as a third column in your comparison spreadsheet before committing — it often wins on net yield versus both.
