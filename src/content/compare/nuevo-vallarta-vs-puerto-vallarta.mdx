## Puerto Vallarta investment profile

Puerto Vallarta predates the Instagram condo era with established expat infrastructure, hospital access, and restaurant walkability that produces among Mexico's strongest Pacific STR review patterns — but hillside stairs, retiree-heavy buildings without STR allowance, and Jalisco municipal registration still kill naive underwriting.

### Zona Romántica (Old Town)

STR liquidity anchor — walkable dining, galleries, beach access via hills or south shore walks. Guest reviews consistently cite restaurant proximity and Malecón evenings.

- Investor fit: Walkable STR operators
- Price band: $300K–450K 1BR
- Net signal: ~4.5%
- HOA: $250–500/month typical
- Risk: Hillside access — test guest path with luggage

### Marina Vallarta and Centro fringe

Marina flights, yacht ecosystem, more car-oriented than Romántica but longer average stays.

- Net signal: ~4%
- Price: $320K–480K
- DD: STR verification essential — mixed HOA quality

### Versalles and value pockets

Local residential colonia with emerging STR — lower ADR, thinner tourist polish, value entry.

- Price: $250K–350K
- Net: ~3.8%
- Fit: Operators accepting lower ADR for basis

### When Puerto Vallarta wins the thesis

Choose Jalisco-side PV when walkability drives reviews, when net yield matters more than resort pool slides, when retiree services and healthcare proximity support long holds, or when entry below $350K matters. Romántica is the benchmark Pacific walkable STR market in Mexico.

## Nuevo Vallarta investment profile

Nuevo Vallarta captures buyers who search "Puerto Vallarta" on OTAs but land in Nayarit resort towers — golf courses, Vidanta-adjacent ecosystems, and marina tourism support family weekly rentals when HOA permits independent STR or favorable rental-pool splits.

### Marina Nuevo Vallarta

Yacht ecosystem, nautical services, waterfront dining — weekly rental potential for boating tourists with longer average stays than Romántica weekend blocks.

- Price: $400K–600K 1BR
- Net: ~3.8–4.2%
- Guest: Families, nautical itineraries

### Golf and resort core

Shared amenity expectations, competitive similar-unit STR, rental pool programmes common.

- Price: $350K–500K
- HOA: $400–700/month
- Net: 3–4%
- Risk: Hotel programme restrictions on independent Airbnb

### Flamingos and north bay fringe

Transition toward Bucerías villages — emerging inventory, variable HOA, thinner liquidity than core Nuevo Vallarta.

- Price: $280K–400K
- Net: 3.5–4.5% indicative

### When Nuevo Vallarta wins the thesis

Choose Nuevo Vallarta when your guest avatar is families wanting pools and golf, when marina weekly blocks fit your calendar, when PVR airport proximity matters for turnover, or when you prefer resort HOA amenities over cobblestone charm — always verify STR independence from rental pool first.

## State, tax, and legal differences

Puerto Vallarta sits in Jalisco; Nuevo Vallarta in Nayarit — same national fideicomiso framework for foreigners in the restricted zone, but municipal STR registration, predial processes, and notario familiarity differ. Use state-appropriate counsel; do not assume Jalisco contracts govern Nayarit closings.

| Topic | Puerto Vallarta (Jalisco) | Nuevo Vallarta (Nayarit) |
|---|---|---|
| Ownership (foreign coastal) | Fideicomiso | Fideicomiso |
| ISAI acquisition tax | ~2–3% indicative | ~2–3% indicative |
| STR registration | PV municipal programs | Bahía de Banderas / Nayarit |
| Legal counsel | Jalisco-savvy | Nayarit-savvy |
| Buyer confusion risk | Low if Romántica named | High — "PV address" marketing |

## STR and HOA operational comparison

Puerto Vallarta Romántica buildings range from STR-friendly boutique stacks to retiree towers that prohibit nightly rentals — zero operational yield if you skip bylaws. Nuevo Vallarta resort HOAs often push rental pool participation with revenue splits that look like gross yield in marketing but net differently on your spreadsheet.

| Operational factor | Puerto Vallarta | Nuevo Vallarta |
|---|---|---|
| Guest car need | Low in Romántica | High |
| Restaurant walk score | High Romántica | Low — drive to dining |
| Rental pool prevalence | Lower | Higher |
| Identical-unit competition | Moderate | High resort stacks |
| Shoulder season | Culinary tourism helps | Family holidays weighted |

Municipal rules change; verify ordinance, HOA, and tax advisor before listing. Bahía de Banderas enforcement on Nayarit side is typically low-medium versus Romántica's established STR ecosystem.

## Risks on each side of the bay

Puerto Vallarta risks include hillside access complaints, hurricane-season occupancy softness (lower than Quintana Roo but not zero), STR municipal tightening, and buying in retiree buildings without STR rights. Nuevo Vallarta risks include rental pool lock-in, confusing Jalisco marketing for Nayarit product, HOA special assessments on pools and golf, and car-dependent guest disappointment.

### Puerto Vallarta red flags

- "Walkable" listing 20 minutes from Romántica on foot
- Retiree HOA with nightly rental prohibition
- Undocumented basis at purchase — ISR shock on exit
- Ignoring stairs and humidity maintenance on hillside units

### Nuevo Vallarta red flags

- Rental pool contract with opaque net split
- STR marketed as Romántica experience — review backlash
- Special assessments on aging resort infrastructure
- Wrong-state legal counsel on Nayarit closing

## Airport and flight access (shared PVR)

Both markets feed Licenciado Gustavo Díaz Ordaz International Airport (PVR) — Nuevo Vallarta often 10–20 minutes, Romántica 15–25 minutes. West-coast US origins dominate; check your city's direct routes seasonally. Airport proximity is a shared advantage — it does not resolve walkability differences for STR guests.

## Resale and liquidity

Puerto Vallarta Romántica resale benefits from global brand recognition and retiree buyer depth — wrong-building mistakes still sell to lifestyle buyers if HOA is clean. Nuevo Vallarta resale competes among similar resort units; differentiation requires marina position, renovation, or rental history proof. Name the state and colonia in listings to avoid buyer confusion.
