## Head-to-head summary

Mexico optimizes for North American STR operators — USD transactions, Cancún airport hub, indicative 4–5% net in Playa walkables. ## Ownership and legal structure

Spain residential purchases typically register direct ownership through notario publico with land registry — familiar to European buyers, no bank trust layer. Foreign buyers from the US face no EU citizenship requirement for most residential apartments and villas outside special military zones.

Mexico coastal restricted zones require fideicomiso — Mexican bank holds title, foreigner is beneficiary for 50-year renewable term. Setup $2,500–$4,000, annual $500–$800. Rights include rent, sell, improve, inherit — standardized with tens of thousands of US transactions annually.

| Layer | Mexico | Spain |
|---|---|---|
| Title instrument | Fideicomiso / direct interior | Escritura + registro |
| Annual carry | Trust fee + predial | IBI + community fees |
| Inheritance | Beneficiary succession | Spanish succession rules apply |
| Ejido / quota risk | Ejido screening essential | Different — verify urbanismo |

## Entry price and closing stack

Mexico investor 1BR in Playa Centro or Mazatlán-type Pacific markets often lists $200,000–320,000 USD. Closing adds ISAI, notary, fideicomiso setup — budget 5–10% all-in.

Spain Costa del Sol and Alicante investor apartments frequently start €250,000–400,000 for walkable or sea-view product. Closing stack includes ITP or VAT on new build, notary, registry, legal — often 10–14% total for resale purchases depending on autonomous community.

Cheaper Mexico sticker does not automatically mean better IRR — model net yield, management, and cross-border tax compliance on both sides.

## STR regulation and operational reality

Mexico STR legality is building-level and municipal — HOA regime de condominio plus Quintana Roo ISH registration and SAT compliance. Professional managers exist in Playa, Cancún, and Mazatlán corridors.

Spain tightened tourist licence requirements in Barcelona, Palma, and several Andalusian municipalities — operating unlicensed STR risks fines and platform delisting. Community rules (comunidad de propietarios) may ban short stays regardless of municipal licence.

| Risk | Mexico | Spain |
|---|---|---|
| HOA / comunidad ban | Common DD failure | Same |
| Municipal licence | ISH + Solidaridad rules | City-specific licence |
| Platform withholding | SAT Airbnb tiers 2026 | Spanish tax reporting separate |
| Manager depth | High in RM | High in Costa del Sol |

## Tax and residency crossover

US citizens owe US tax on worldwide income regardless of property location. Mexico rental triggers ISR reporting and potential withholding; sale triggers ISR withholding at closing with CFDI basis documentation.

Spain non-resident landlords typically face Spanish non-resident income tax on rental — often cited as 24% flat rate on gross for EU non-residents without permanent establishment, verify treaty and current Agencia Tributaria guidance. Wealth tax and imputed income rules on secondary homes affect non-resident owners in some regions.

Golden Visa note: Spain effectively removed real-estate investment routes for new Golden Visa applicants in 2025 legislative changes — do not underwrite residency on a €500K apartment purchase without current Spanish immigration counsel.

## Liquidity and exit buyer pool

Mexico Riviera Maya and Los Cabos exit pools skew US and Canadian — roughly 65% US share of foreign buyers nationally per industry citing. Resale liquidity concentrates in walkable Playa and branded Cabos towers.

Spain coastal resale pools skew European — British, German, Nordic, and domestic Spanish buyers. US sellers may face longer marketing periods unless pricing for EU buyer preferences and energy certificate requirements.

Appreciation neither market guarantees — Mexico 2025 Quintana Roo state-level citing showed strong growth with colonia bifurcation; Spain post-2022 euro markets cooled from peak in several coastal zones.

## Buyer scenarios

Scenario A — US STR cash-flow buyer: Mexico Playa or Mazatlán wins on net yield math and manager depth. Spain only if licensed STR in a municipality you verified personally.

Scenario B — US buyer wanting EU base: Spain may win on lifestyle and direct title despite lower net yield — budget legal and tax stack before comparing sticker prices.

Scenario C — Portfolio diversifier: Holding both adds operational load — two managers, two tax systems, two HOA cultures. Most retail buyers should master one market first.
