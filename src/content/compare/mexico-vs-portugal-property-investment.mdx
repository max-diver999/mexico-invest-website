## Golden Visa and residency — critical 2026 context

Portugal's Golden Visa program removed most real-estate qualifying routes in 2023 legislative changes — purchases in Lisbon, Porto, coastal Algarve, and most high-demand municipalities generally no longer count toward residency investment thresholds. Remaining eligible routes may include interior regions, certain fund investments, or other non-residential categories — verify current AIMA published list before any residency-linked purchase.

Mexico does not grant residency through property price thresholds. Temporary and permanent resident visas follow separate immigration criteria — property ownership may support financial solvency evidence but is not a €500K-style golden ticket.

| Goal | Mexico path | Portugal path |
|---|---|---|
| Buy beach condo + STR | Standard fideicomiso | Direct title + licence DD |
| Residency via real estate | Not price-linked | Mostly closed on prime coast |
| US snowbird 6 months | Visitor/temporary routes | Schengen 90/180 rules for US |

## Ownership structure

Portugal residential purchases register direct freehold through notary and land registry — familiar structure without bank trust layer. Condominium rules govern short-stay restrictions in many buildings.

Mexico coastal restricted zones use fideicomiso — 50-year renewable bank trust with beneficiary rights to rent, sell, improve, inherit. Annual trust fees $500–800 plus predial.

## Tax stack for US citizens

US citizens report Schedule E rental income and eventual capital gains regardless of property country. Double taxation mitigation may use Form 1116 foreign tax credits where Mexican or Portuguese tax paid — consult cross-border CPA.

Mexico: ISR on rental, SAT registration for active STR, withholding on platform bookings in 2026 enforcement wave. Sale: ISR withholding at closing with CFDI cost basis.

Portugal: Rental income taxed under Portuguese rules for non-residents or residents. NHR 10-year preferential regime was substantially narrowed in 2024 reforms — do not underwrite purchases on expired NHR marketing decks.

## STR licensing and operations

Mexico STR compliance stacks HOA regime de condominio, municipal ISH in Quintana Roo, SAT RFC and withholding — see [Short-Term Rental Rules Riviera Maya](/guides/short-term-rental-rules-riviera-maya/) and [Airbnb Investment Mexico Guide](/guides/airbnb-investment-mexico-guide/).

Portugal requires local lodging registration (AL licence) in many municipalities — Lisbon and coastal zones tightened supply caps. Condominium assemblies increasingly restrict short stays. Operating without AL where required risks fines.

| Operational factor | Mexico RM | Portugal coastal |
|---|---|---|
| US guest demand | Very high | Moderate |
| Manager supply | Deep Playa/Cancún | Deep Algarve |
| Licence cap risk | Municipal | Municipal + EU tourism policy |
| Platform tax data | SAT 2026 tiers | AT reporting |

## Entry price and closing economics

Mexico Playa walkable 1BR: $250,000–350,000 USD typical. Mazatlán Centro-type: $180,000–280,000. Closing 5–10%.

Portugal Algarve or Lisbon investor 1BR: €300,000–550,000+ typical in walkable zones. IMT transfer tax scales with value — budget 6–10% all-in including legal and registry.

Portugal resale pools skew EU — British, French, German, domestic Portuguese. US sellers may need longer marketing and energy certificate compliance (CE).

## Currency and financing note

Mexico resort deals often price in USD in Riviera Maya and Mazatlán corridors — simplifying US buyer pro formas. Portugal transactions settle in euros — US buyers face FX translation on entry, carry costs, and eventual repatriation. Neither FX direction is guaranteed favorable; stress-test peso or euro moves against your holding period.

Mortgage access for non-residents remains limited in both countries — cash buyers dominate foreign volume. Developer financing in Mexico pre-construction carries separate escrow discipline — see [Pre-Construction Mexico Risks](/guides/pre-construction-mexico-risks/).
