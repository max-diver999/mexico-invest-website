    answer: "1BR branded and beach-proximate condos commonly range $450,000–900,000+ USD in 2026. Ultra-luxury villas exceed $2M. Closing via fideicomiso adds 5–10%. HOA fees often run $800–1,200+ per month on luxury stacks."
  - question: "What rental yields can Corridor investors expect?"
    answer: "Gross yields of 5.5–6% appear in marketing. Net yields after 28–30% management and high HOA commonly compress to roughly 2.5–3.8% on branded 1BR product. Many owners rent 12–20 weeks to offset carrying costs rather than maximise cash yield."
  - question: "Is the Cabo Corridor good for Airbnb?"
    answer: "STR occurs in branded residences with hotel-style management, ADR can be strong in winter. Execution requires premium furnishing and service levels. Verify STR rules per regime; some stacks favour hotel rental pools over independent Airbnb."
  - question: "Corridor vs San José del Cabo for investment?"
    answer: "San José centro offers walkability and often better net yields near 3.8–4.5%. Corridor offers luxury branding, golf, and beach clubs with lower net near 2.5–3.8%. Match zone to guest budget and owner-use thesis."
  - question: "Can foreigners buy on the Cabo Corridor?"
    answer: "Yes via fideicomiso. Foreign buyers dominate luxury corridor sales. Independent legal counsel and HOA due diligence are critical, special assessments on luxury amenities are common."
  - question: "What are main risks on the Cabo Corridor?"
    answer: "HOA fee escalation, low net yield if purchased on gross marketing, overbuilding in generic luxury towers, water utility costs, and long DOM on overpriced view units without rental history."

    answer: "Both offer branded coastal product. Riviera Maya luxury sits in RM supply waves (Tulum oversupply caution). Corridor luxury trades on Baja scarcity and US west-coast flights with typically lower net yield than Playa Centro walkable product."
