**Quick answer:** The Cabo Corridor is Los Cabos' luxury resort strip between San José and Cabo San Lucas — **$450K–900K+** 1BR branded inventory, **2.5–3.8% net yields** after high HOA, golf and beach-club tourism. Foreign fideicomiso ownership. Thesis is premium USD asset and owner-use offset — not maximum cash yield.

Punta Ballena, Palmilla, and twin coastlines along the Transpeninsular define Mexico's desert-meets-luxury-resort category — car-based, HOA-heavy, ADR-strong in winter.

## Key zones along the Transpeninsular

**Punta Ballena offers the most balanced combination of price entry and rental potential along the Corridor, while Palmilla commands ultra-luxury premiums that rarely justify the net yield sacrifice compared to more accessible Corridor locations.**

### Punta Ballena / Médano

Whale-watching geography, resort clusters, ocean views. Mixed vintage and new towers.

- **Price:** $500K–800K 1BR
- **HOA:** $700–1,100/month
- **Guest:** Resort family, golf

### Palmilla

Historic luxury enclave — golf, beach club culture, established USD buyer pool.

- **Price:** $600K–1M+ 1BR
- **Net:** Often under 3.5%
- **Guest:** High-net-worth leisure

### Chileno Bay / Cabo del Sol

Master-planned luxury — newer phases, strict architectural codes.

- **Investor fit:** Second-home + hotel-pool rent
- **DD:** Builder and HOA health critical

### Corridors fringe toward San José or CSL

Transition zones — verify exact colonia; "Corridor" marketing spans 30 km.

## HOA and branded management reality

**Corridor HOA fees typically range $800–1,200+ monthly for luxury amenities including pools, beach clubs, security, and desert landscaping — special assessments for facade and infrastructure upgrades are routine and can add $10,000–30,000 per unit unexpectedly.**

Corridor towers often include:

- Pool and beach club maintenance
- Landscaping in desert climate
- 24-hour security
- Concierge expectations
- Reserve funds for desalination and common-area upgrades

**Special assessments** for facade, pool resurfacing, and elevator modernisation are routine — request 3-year financial history.

Some branded stacks route rentals through hotel rental pools — independent Airbnb may be restricted or fee-heavy.

## Infrastructure

**Desert Corridor infrastructure requires careful water supply verification including municipal connections and backup systems — pool-heavy luxury developments stress water resources, while transportation relies heavily on Transpeninsular Highway access subject to holiday traffic bottlenecks.**

**Water:** Critical diligence item — municipal plus backup wells; pool-heavy HOAs stress supply.

**Power:** Generally reliable; backup generators in premium stacks.

**Roads:** Transpeninsular — accident and holiday traffic affect guest transfers.

**Retail:** Resort-centric; car required for most guest profiles.

## Risks 2026

**Primary 2026 risks include net yield illusions where gross marketing hides 2% actual returns after HOA, new luxury supply competition, extended days-on-market for overpriced inventory, salt-air maintenance costs, and narrow resale buyer pools for ultra-luxury properties.**

**Net yield illusion:** Gross marketing on $900K view units hides 2% net after HOA.

**Supply:** New luxury phases add competition — differentiate or discount.

**DOM at top end:** Overpriced inventory without rental track record sits.

**Climate maintenance:** Salt air and desert stress common areas — capex reserves matter.

**Resale buyer pool:** Narrower than San José walkable — price for proven pro forma.

## Due diligence field trip checklist

When visiting before offer, block two full days minimum:

**Day 1 — Physical:** Walk guest path from unit to primary attraction (beach, dining, marina). Test at night. Check pool, parking, elevator, water pressure, AC noise.

**Day 2 — Professional:** Interview two property managers with local portfolio. Meet attorney for 30-minute scope call. Request HOA STR confirmation letter template from seller.

Bring spreadsheet with net yield sensitivity — occupancy minus 10% and ADR minus 15% stress cases. If stress case still acceptable, proceed; if not, renegotiate or pass.
