## Colonia guide for investors

**Zona Romántica provides the highest probability of STR success in Puerto Vallarta due to walkable restaurant density and established guest review patterns, while Marina offers longer stays but lower net yields due to higher entry costs.**

### Zona Romántica (Old Town)

STR liquidity anchor — walkable dining, galleries, beach access via hills or south walk. Guest reviews consistently cite restaurant proximity.

- **Investor fit:** STR operators
- **Net signal:** ~4.5%
- **HOA:** $250–500/month typical
- **Risk:** Hillside stairs — test guest path

### Centro / Emiliano Zapata fringe

Transition zones between hotel row and Romántica — varying HOA quality.

- **Net:** 3.8–4.5%
- **DD:** STR verification essential

### Marina Vallarta

Marina flights, yacht ecosystem, more car-oriented than Romántica.

- **Price:** $320K–480K
- **Net:** ~4%
- **Guest:** Nautical, moderate-length stays

### Versalles

Local residential, emerging STR — value entry with less tourist polish.

- **Price:** $250K–350K
- **Net:** ~3.8%
- **Fit:** Value operators accepting lower ADR

### Conchas Chinas / South Shore

Premium hillside — views, privacy, access friction.

- **Price:** $450K–700K+
- **Net:** 3–4%
- **Fit:** Lifestyle + selective rent

### Hotel Zone (north)

Institutional tourism — different STR dynamics than Romántica walkable.

## Retiree ecosystem vs STR thesis

**Puerto Vallarta's established retiree population creates potential conflicts with STR operations — buildings with growing retiree majorities may vote to restrict nightly rentals, making STR-tolerant building selection critical for investment success.** PV uniquely blends retiree owner-occupiers and STR operators in the same bay. Buildings trending retiree may restrict nightly rentals — read regime de condominio and meeting minutes for anti-STR votes.

If rental is your thesis, prioritise STR-tolerant buildings in Romántica over quiet retiree towers with low fees but zero rentability.

## Due diligence field trip checklist

When visiting before offer, block two full days minimum:

**Day 1 — Physical:** Walk the guest route from your unit to the Malecón and Los Muertos Beach, counting staircases on hillside routes — anything over 80 steps without elevator loses family and senior bookings. Visit during a rainy-season afternoon to check street drainage and lobby flooding; colonial-era Romántica buildings charm guests visually but can pool water at entry level if drainage is poor. Test evening noise from nearby restaurants with live music if your unit faces a dining street.

**Day 2 — Professional:** Interview two Zona Romántica managers and ask how cruise-port days affect short-stay bookings and neighbourhood congestion. Meet a Jalisco-licensed attorney for scope on STR municipal registration — Jalisco rules differ from Nayarit across the river, and confusing the two creates compliance gaps. Request HOA meeting minutes to check whether retiree-majority votes have restricted or are trending toward restricting nightly rentals.

Bring a spreadsheet with net yield sensitivity using Romántica ADR of $180–280/night in winter and $100–160/night in summer — stress occupancy at 62% to account for July–October rainy-season softness and ADR minus 15%. If the stress case still produces 3%+ net after HOA $250–500/month, the walkable thesis holds; if not, compare Versalles entry pricing before passing entirely.

## Zona Romántica vs Marina Vallarta for investors

**Zona Romántica** (Old Town south shore) commands walkable dining and cobblestone charm — higher ADR for boutique STR when HOA permits. Parking and hills affect guest logistics; verify noise ordinances on weekend nights.

**Marina Vallarta** offers yacht-club adjacency and golf — longer-stay guests, lower turn frequency. Net yields can trail Romántica on price-per-metre but with smoother shoulder-season occupancy from snowbird patterns.

**Versalles and Fluvial** inland pockets trade beach premium for larger floor plans — better for owner-occupier weeks with moderate rent, not peak STR arbitrage.

Match colonia to guest profile before comparing headline prices across PV sub-markets.

Flight access from US west coast (LAX, PHX, SFO, SEA) and Texas hubs supports both owner use and STR guest turnover — a structural advantage versus more remote Mexican micro-markets with thinner airline schedules.
