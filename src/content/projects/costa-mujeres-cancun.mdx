## Ownership and closing for foreigners

Mexico's Constitution restricts direct foreign ownership of coastal land. Foreign buyers use fideicomiso, a 50-year renewable bank trust where a Mexican bank holds title as trustee and the foreign buyer holds full beneficial rights, including the right to rent, sell, gift, or bequeath the property.

| Closing item | $285K purchase | $720K purchase |
|---|---|---|
| ISAI transfer tax 2–3% | $5,700–8,550 | $14,400–21,600 |
| Notary and registry | $4,275–7,125 | $10,800–18,000 |
| Fideicomiso setup | $2,500–4,000 | $2,500–4,000 |
| Legal review | $1,500–3,000 | $2,000–4,000 |
| Total closing | ~$14K–23K | ~$30K–48K |

Fideicomiso annual maintenance runs $500–700/year after formation. Remote closing via power of attorney is available, standard for pre-construction offshore buyers. Legal guide: [Fideicomiso Mexico Explained](/guides/fideicomiso-mexico-explained/).
