## Palmilla Bay: the swimmable cove advantage

Los Cabos' Corridor beaches face open Pacific swells that make many beaches surfable but not family-swimmable. Palmilla Bay is a protected crescent where calm water allows year-round swimming, snorkeling, and paddleboarding. For luxury villa rental marketing, swimmable beachfront in Los Cabos commands 20–35% ADR premium over surf-exposed beachfront.

| Beach type | Water condition | Family appeal | ADR premium |
|---|---|---|---|
| Pacific-exposed surf beach | Rough, advisory signs | Limited | Base |
| Sea of Cortez beach | Calm, warm | High | +10–15% |
| Protected cove (Palmilla) | Calm Pacific, swimmable | Very high | +20–35% |

Palmilla Bay's cove geometry creates this calm-water condition on the Pacific side — an extremely rare combination of Pacific location and protected swimming conditions. For families with children booking luxury villas, this distinction is decisive.

## Rental economics in the Palmilla zone

Palmilla Bay's combination of protected beach, historical prestige, and One&Only resort adjacency supports ADR at the top of the Los Cabos rental market.

| Villa type | Nightly rate range | Annual booked nights | Gross yield estimate |
|---|---|---|---|
| Entry corridor residence (3BR) | $1,500–$2,500 | 55–70 | 8–12% gross |
| Bay-access villa (4BR, pool) | $2,500–$4,000 | 60–80 | 9–13% gross |
| Oceanfront villa (5BR) | $3,500–$6,000 | 70–90 | 11–15% gross |

After 30–35% management fee, HOA ($1,500–$2,800/month for luxury properties), utilities, and maintenance reserves, net yields land approximately 4–6% across the range. High gross yields at luxury price points compress to moderate net yields after the full cost stack.

At $720K with conservative modeling (55 nights, $1,800/night average): $99,000 gross, $69,300 after 30% management, $51,500 after HOA and costs — 7.2% net. Stronger performance is achievable but depends on villa branding and management quality.

## Due diligence: Palmilla-specific considerations

Palmilla corridor properties carry specific diligence requirements beyond standard pre-construction checks, reflecting the complex historical land ownership, resort adjacency, and multi-phase masterplan governance.

| Diligence item | Palmilla-specific requirement |
|---|---|
| Beach access easement | Verify in escritura, not just marketing |
| One&Only adjacency rights | Confirm what amenities are shared vs. resort-exclusive |
| HOA structure | Multi-phase masterplan with complex governance |
| Water infrastructure | Private system, verify capacity and reliability |
| Environmental permits | Corridor construction requires SEMARNAT review |
| Masterplan phase | Where in development sequence, completion risk |
