## Lerma: the Gulf coast village next to a colonial city

Lerma is not a resort strip — it is a working fishing village and coastal suburb of Campeche city with authentic Mexican character. The village sits roughly 7 km south of Campeche's historic center along the Gulf coast highway, offering direct beach access to the Gulf of Mexico's calm, warm waters. The area is known locally for its shrimp market, fresh seafood restaurants, and accessible weekend beach scene for Campeche and Mérida residents.

| Access point | Drive time (indicative) |
|---|---|
| Campeche historic center (UNESCO) | 15–20 min |
| Campeche airport (CPE) | 15–25 min |
| Mérida (domestic tourism) | 2.5–3 hrs |
| Villahermosa | 3–4 hrs |
| Mexico City (flight) | ~2 hrs from CPE |

The Gulf of Mexico's character differs from the Caribbean: water runs calmer and greener, excellent for swimming and family beach use. The absence of coral reef provides a different underwater profile but removes the Caribbean's reef-snorkeling tourism driver. STR guest profiling should emphasize: family beach retreat, seafood and culture, UNESCO heritage day-trip base.

## Environmental and regulatory requirements for beachfront

Beachfront development in Mexico carries additional regulatory layers beyond standard municipal permits. Lerma buyers must confirm their developer has secured all required authorizations before depositing.

| Permit type | Issuing authority | Critical check |
|---|---|---|
| Building permit | Municipio de Campeche | Verified at municipal offices |
| Environmental impact (MIA) | SEMARNAT | Written authorization required |
| Federal maritime zone | SEMARNAT / ZOFEMAT | Setback boundary confirmed |
| Title search | Notario público | Ejido-free coastal parcel |
| Fideicomiso trust | Named bank | Contract must specify |

The zona federal marítimo terrestre (ZOFEMAT) is a federal maritime zone that extends 20 meters inland from the high tide line. No permanent structures are permitted within this zone. Verify the development's footprint does not encroach on ZOFEMAT and that SEMARNAT MIA authorization covers the specific parcel.

Lerma beachfront sits within Mexico's restricted zone. All foreign buyers require fideicomiso bank trust to hold title legally.

| Closing item | $173K purchase (indicative) |
|---|---|
| ISAI transfer tax 2–3% | $3,460–$5,190 |
| Notary + public registry | $2,595–$4,325 |
| Fideicomiso setup | $2,500–$4,000 |
| Environmental due diligence | $1,000–$2,000 |
| Legal review | $1,500–$3,000 |
| Total | ~$11,055–$18,515 |

Timeline from signed contract to registered fideicomiso: typically 45–90 days. Remote closing is available via notarized power of attorney — standard for foreign buyers.

## Due diligence checklist for Lerma beachfront

Before depositing on Lerma Beach Condos Campeche:

1. Title search: notario confirms escritura clear of ejido, ZOFEMAT setback compliant.
2. Building permit: licencia de construcción at municipio de Campeche confirmed.
3. SEMARNAT MIA: environmental impact authorization in hand for the specific parcel.
4. ZOFEMAT survey: independent survey confirming 20m setback boundary.
5. Hurricane insurance: verify coverage availability and annual premium before commitment.
6. Escrow: milestone structure — no more than 10–15% before foundation poured.
7. HOA pro forma: 5-year projection with storm reserve fund.
8. STR authorization: written HOA permission for short-term rental.
9. Comps: check Lerma Airbnb listings for realistic ADR benchmarks.
10. Attorney review: refund terms, default provisions, delivery penalty clearly stated.
