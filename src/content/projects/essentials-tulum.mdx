## Tulum STR Market: Entry-Tier Performance

Tulum's short-term rental market remains one of Mexico's strongest. Entry condos in accessible locations consistently achieve competitive occupancy when professionally managed.

| STR metric | Peak season (Nov–Apr) | Shoulder (May–Oct) | Annual average |
|---|---|---|---|
| Average occupancy | 72–80% | 50–62% | 62–70% |
| ADR (1BR entry unit) | $180–$250 | $100–$140 | $145–$175 |
| Gross revenue (1BR) | $32K–$40K | — | $32K–$38K |
| Management fee | 25–30% | — | — |
| Net yield estimate | — | — | 4–6% |

Entry-tier performance is more sensitive to management quality than premium-tier units. A design-led property can sustain occupancy on brand recognition; an entry unit depends on pricing optimization, fast response time, and review scores. Budget for professional management from day one.

## Pre-Construction Risk Framework

Off-plan investment at entry price points concentrates risk differently than premium product:

| Risk factor | Entry-tier risk | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Developer capacity | Higher — smaller operators | Verify prior completions personally |
| Finish quality | Medium — budget constraints | Inspect model unit or prior project |
| Delivery timeline | Medium | Escrow milestone schedule, penalties |
| Title / ejido | Same as all Tulum product | Attorney ejido-free verification |
| HOA adequacy | Higher — smaller reserves | Review HOA pro forma for 5-year period |
| STR competition | Medium — commoditized segment | Professional management, strong photography |

For entry buyers, developer track record due diligence is more critical than for premium projects, because budget developers have less margin for error. Prior-completed project inspections are non-negotiable. Full pre-con checklist: [Pre-Construction Mexico Risks](/guides/pre-construction-mexico-risks/).

## Pre-Purchase Checklist

1. Attorney verification of ejido-free title: absolutely mandatory in Tulum jungle zone.
2. Construction permit (licencia de construcción): confirm with municipio of Tulum.
3. MIA environmental clearance: required for jungle-zone development.
4. Third-party escrow: milestone releases tied to construction progress, not sales targets.
5. Finishes schedule: signed as contract attachment, not brochure insert.
6. HOA pro forma: 5-year projection, reserve fund structure.
7. STR allowance: confirm HOA rules explicitly permit short-term rental.
8. Developer inspection: visit a prior Essence Tulum project and inspect finish delivery quality.
