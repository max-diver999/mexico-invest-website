## Foreign Ownership: Fideicomiso Structure

Foreign buyers in Mexico's restricted coastal zone hold property through a fideicomiso (bank trust), typically through BBVA, Banorte, or Banamex. The trust holds legal title; the foreign buyer holds full beneficial rights including rental income, resale, and inheritance.

| Fideicomiso line item | Cost estimate |
|---|---|
| Trust setup | $2,500–$4,000 |
| Annual trustee fee | $500–$800 |
| Trust term | 50 years, renewable |
| Beneficiary change | $300–$500 |

Remote closing via notarized power of attorney is standard for off-plan purchases. The fideicomiso should reference STR rights explicitly — some older trust templates restrict commercial use.

Tulum has no dedicated STR registration ordinance as of June 2026 (Quintana Roo framework applies), but HOA approval is decisive. Municipal tax registration (RFC and hospedaje tax 3%) is required.

## Pre-Purchase Checklist

1. Verify construction permit at Tulum municipality — not just developer confirmation.
2. Request title search from independent notario confirming no ejido claim.
3. Confirm MIA (environmental impact) permit for jungle-zone builds.
4. Review escrow account terms: notarial escrow or trust account, not developer bank account.
5. Obtain HOA pro forma for 5 years including reserves.
6. Confirm STR permission in HOA rules.
7. Request comparable STR revenue data from operating units in same zone.
8. Attorney review of promissory contract, delay penalty terms, and refund conditions.
