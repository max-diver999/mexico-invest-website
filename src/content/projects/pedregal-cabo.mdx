## Los Cabos luxury market context

Los Cabos ranked as Mexico's highest-value real estate market by transaction volume in the $500K+ category for 2023–2025 per industry data, with foreign buyer share above 60% in the luxury hillside tier. The combination of direct US flight access, year-round desert climate, Michelin-adjacent dining, and world-class sportfishing creates a demand profile unmatched elsewhere in Mexico.

| Los Cabos market segment | Price tier | Foreign buyer % |
|---|---|---|
| Entry condo (downtown SJC) | $250K–$500K | 35–40% |
| Mid luxury (Corridor) | $500K–$900K | 50–60% |
| Luxury hillside | $890K–$2.4M | 65–75% |
| Trophy estate | Over $3M | 80–90% |

Hillside properties in the $890K–$2.4M tier have appreciated 35–55% from 2019 to 2025 per Los Cabos Association of REALTORS data, outperforming beach-level luxury on a percentage basis. Constrained hillside topography limits supply growth in a way that beachfront projects cannot replicate.

## Capital appreciation thesis

Los Cabos hillside property appreciation outperforms Mexico averages for structural reasons: desert geography limits buildable hillside land supply, US buyer demand is income-driven (not inflation-correlated), and the luxury hospitality ecosystem continuously deepens with Nobu, Rosewood, Four Seasons, and independent Michelin-level dining.

| Appreciation driver | Los Cabos hillside advantage |
|---|---|
| Land supply constraint | Topography limits hillside inventory |
| US buyer demand | Dollar-denominated, income-driven |
| Brand anchor growth | 4–5 star resort openings increase comp base |
| Airport expansion | SJD capacity growing, more direct routes |
| Golf infrastructure | Quivira, Diamante, Querencia anchor demand |

Hillside properties purchased in 2018–2020 at $550K–$900K sold in 2023–2025 at $900K–$1.6M per comparable transaction data. This appreciation is not guaranteed to repeat but reflects structural supply constraints. Full market guide: [Los Cabos Property Investment Guide](/guides/los-cabos-property-investment-guide/).

## Villa rental economics at $890K–$2.4M

Luxury villa rental generates high absolute dollars at moderate yield percentage. A $1.3M villa generating $4,000/night at 55 annual booked nights (15% occupancy) produces $220,000 gross annually — 16.9% gross — but after 30% management fee, HOA at $1,800/month, utilities, and maintenance reserves, net is approximately 7–9%.

At conservative 30 booked nights at $3,000/night: $90,000 gross, $63,000 after management, $41,400 after HOA and costs — 3.2% net yield on $1.3M. Luxury villa rental income requires active marketing to the corporate retreat and high-net-worth leisure segment, not passive Airbnb management.

| Scenario | Nightly rate | Booked nights | Net yield |
|---|---|---|---|
| Conservative | $2,800 | 30 | 3.1% |
| Base case | $3,500 | 45 | 5.2% |
| Strong year | $4,500 | 60 | 7.6% |

## Ownership structure for luxury foreign buyers

Fideicomiso at luxury price points operates identically to lower tiers but with higher closing cost absolutes. Title insurance — uncommon at lower price points — becomes cost-effective and recommended at $700K+ purchase prices.

| Closing cost | $890K purchase |
|---|---|
| ISAI (2–3%) | $17,800–$26,700 |
| Notary + public registry | $13,350–$22,250 |
| Fideicomiso setup | $3,000–$5,000 |
| Attorney review | $4,000–$8,000 |
| Title insurance (optional) | $3,000–$6,000 |
| Total | ~$41K–$68K |
