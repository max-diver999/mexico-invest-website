## What the June 2026 passenger data shows

ASUR-operated Cancún hub data through mid-2026 showed continued post-pandemic normalization with US and Canadian route density at the core. Indicative H1 2026 passenger throughput exceeded 6.2 million at Cancún alone, not counting Cozumel or Chetumal feeders.

| Signal (Jun 2026) | Indicative level | Investor takeaway |
|---|---|---|
| Cancún H1 passengers | 6.2M+ | STR demand floor stays elevated |
| Top origin markets | US, Canada, domestic MX | Matches foreign buyer profile |
| Playa drive time | 45–60 min from CUN | Airport feeds Playa occupancy |
| Tulum transfer | 90–120 min | Higher execution risk, higher gross in select zones |
| Hotel Zone ticket | $400K+ 1BR common | Appreciation play over volume STR |

Passenger records do not repeal HOA bans on nightly rentals. They also do not fix Tulum Region 15 oversupply where DOM stretched past 70 days on weak inventory.

## From runway to rental: where demand converts

Three conversion paths matter for property investors:

1. **Hotel Zone and Puerto Cancún**: branded luxury, lower STR volume per square foot, higher ticket sizes.
2. **Playa del Carmen corridor**: volume STR liquidity, 1BR tickets often $180K–320K, net yields indicative 4.3–5.2% on managed units.
3. **Tulum south**: marketing-heavy gross yields with colonia bifurcation; buyer leverage rising on stale towers.

## Foreign buyer flow: airport stats vs closing tables

Industry sources citing 40,000+ annual foreign purchases still place US buyers at roughly 65% share. Cancún's route map mirrors that profile, Dallas, Houston, Miami, Toronto, Montreal.

Entry tickets for fideicomiso condos in walkable Playa zones start near $130,000 USD on selective resale inventory; new development stacks run higher. Annual trust fees typically land $550–$800 depending on bank and parcel value.

## STR underwriting checklist for hub-driven demand

Before treating passenger records as a green light:

| Check | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| HOA minutes (24 mo) | STR bans survive tourism booms |
| Municipal lodging registration | Fines stack regardless of occupancy |
| Trailing-12 P&L | Pro-forma decks ignore cleaning and channel fees |
| Management contract | 20–35% gross is normal in Riviera Maya |
| Net at 50% occupancy | Stress test beats headline season |

## Practical playbook for June 2026 buyers

1. Match colonia to guest profile: business-leisure mix in Playa Centro differs from boho Tulum Instagram demand.
2. Demand proof of permitted STR operation, not only Airbnb calendar screenshots.
3. Model net yield after management, HOA, predial, and SAT compliance costs.
4. If exit in 3–5 years matters, prioritize DOM under 45 days in documented buildings.
5. Keep [Due Diligence Mexico Real Estate](/guides/due-diligence-mexico-real-estate/) open through notario week.

Tourism records support the demand story. They do not replace building-level diligence.
