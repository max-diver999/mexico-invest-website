## July 2026 fee snapshot

| Bank (indicative) | Setup USD | Annual USD | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Scotiabank | $2,800–3,800 | $600–800 | Common in QR/BCS |
| BBVA Mexico | $2,500–3,600 | $550–750 | Strong wire desk |
| Banorte | $2,600–3,700 | $580–780 | Wide coastal footprint |
| Intercam | $2,700–4,000 | $650–850 | Premium service tier |

SRE federal permit and notario lines add separately; see [Cost of Buying Property](/guides/cost-of-buying-property-mexico/).

## Why annual fees matter for STR investors

A $700/year trust fee on a $250K Playa 1BR targeting 4.5% net is roughly 0.28% of value annually, small next to management, but ignored in gross yield decks.

## Buyer checklist before signing trust agreement

1. Confirm annual fee tier for your exact purchase price
2. Ask renewal process and 50-year extension cost
3. Verify succession processing timeline and fees
4. Compare wire instructions for closing vs post-closing peso bills
5. Open local account if paying predial in MXN: [Mexican Bank Account Guide](/guides/mexican-bank-account-foreign-property-owner/)

## Renewal and succession fees to model

Buyers focused on setup price sometimes miss renewal quotes at year 45–50 and succession processing when naming heirs. Indicative succession fees run $800–1,500 USD at major banks, coordinate with [Estate Planning for US Citizens](/guides/estate-planning-will-mexico-property-us-citizen/) before closing.

On resale, the buyer may assume the existing trust or establish a new one, assignment fees vary. Sellers should request a payoff-style trust statement early to avoid closing delays.
