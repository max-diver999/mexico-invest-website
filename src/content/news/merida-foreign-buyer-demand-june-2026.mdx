## Direct title vs fideicomiso: what changes at closing

Foreign buyers in the restricted zone, Cancún, Playa, Tulum, Los Cabos, use fideicomiso bank trusts. Mérida's inland location allows direct ownership for standard residential parcels.

| Item | Mérida (direct title) | Riviera Maya (fideicomiso) |
|---|---|---|
| Ownership structure | Escritura in buyer name | Bank trust beneficiary |
| Annual recurring | Predial, HOA | + trust fee $550–$800 |
| Closing complexity | Standard notario | Trust setup + notario |
| STR volume | Moderate | High in Playa |
| Ejido risk | Still present if improper | Still present if improper |

Trust mechanics explained: [Fideicomiso Mexico Explained](/guides/fideicomiso-mexico-explained/). Step-by-step: [Buy Property as a Foreigner](/guides/buy-property-mexico-foreigner/).

Mérida investors often underwrite long-term leases to expat professionals and medical tourism adjacencies, not 180-night STR calendars.

## Due diligence red flags (unchanged by direct title)

Direct title does not cure:

1. Ejido or communal land sold as "about to regularize"
2. Missing predial receipts or frozen certificates
3. Historic Centro facade restrictions blocking renovation plans
4. Seller-promised rental yields without tenant contracts

Checklist: [Due Diligence Mexico Real Estate](/guides/due-diligence-mexico-real-estate/) · [Mistakes Foreign Buyers Make](/guides/mistakes-foreign-buyers-mexico/).
