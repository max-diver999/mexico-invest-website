Quick answer: Foreign owner RFC appointments in the Playa-Cancún SAT corridor ran 3–5 weeks out in late June 2026, contadores say, as STR owners respond to 20% Airbnb withholding. Passport–fideicomiso name mismatches remain the top reason appointments fail on first visit.

Six Playa-area contadores serving US and Canadian STR owners reported **longer SAT queues** in the final week of June 2026, coinciding with Airbnb withholding notices and municipal ISH registration pushes in Solidaridad.

## Appointment wait times (contador reports)

| Period | Foreign owner RFC wait | First-visit success rate |
|---|---:|---:|
| Q1 2025 | 1–2 weeks | ~85% |
| Q4 2025 | 2–3 weeks | ~78% |
| June 2026 | 3–5 weeks | ~71% |

Sample: six contador interviews, not official SAT published data.

---

## Top failure reasons on appointment day

| Issue | Frequency | Fix timeline |
|---|---:|---|
| Passport name ≠ fideicomiso beneficiary | 38% | 5–10 business days |
| Missing CURP or wrong immigration form | 22% | 1–3 days |
| Wrong actividad económica selected | 14% | Same-week correction |
| No apostille on POA | 12% | 2–4 weeks |
| Owner booked wrong SAT module | 9% | Rebook 3–5 weeks |

Managers offering RFC escort services without document pre-review **increase** failure rates, contadores report, owners arrive with incomplete packets.

## What owners should do now

1. Start RFC **before** first booking, not after 90 days of withholding
2. Pay contador for 48-hour document pre-review
3. Book morning slots when available
4. Bring duplicate printed sets plus USB PDFs
5. Update Airbnb tax profile same week RFC activates

ISH layer after RFC: [Quintana Roo Lodging Tax Registration](/guides/quintana-roo-lodging-tax-registration-guide/).

## US tax mirror still required

RFC does not replace US Schedule E or Form 1116. Owners who fix Mexico withholding but ignore US reporting create dual exposure, coordinate Mexican contador and US CPA in the same month RFC goes live.
