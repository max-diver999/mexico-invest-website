## Why investors target Los Cabos

Los Cabos attracts foreign capital because of its luxury tourism brand, US proximity for West Coast buyers, modern resort-residential stock, and premium ADR potential in select corridors. The flip side: high entry costs from USD 350,000, substantial HOA fees that often exceed USD 800 per month, and hurricane exposure that creates seasonal vacancy risk. It is a lifestyle-investment blend where net yield takes secondary priority to brand and location prestige.

| Factor | Los Cabos edge |
|---|---|
| Luxury brand | Premium ADR potential |
| US proximity | West Coast buyer flow |
| Marina lifestyle | Walkable dining/nightlife |
| Resort residential | Amenity-rich buildings |
| Airport access | Direct US flights |

| Factor | Los Cabos challenge |
|---|---|
| Entry cost | **$350K+ realistic** |
| HOA | **$400–1,200/mo** common |
| Hurricane risk | **Sep–Nov exposure** |
| Water supply | Development constraints |
| Luxury focus | Lower net yields |

## Corridor investment zones

Los Cabos splits into five investment corridors with distinct risk-return profiles. Cabo San Lucas marina offers the best STR management infrastructure and walkable appeal for mid-luxury condos. San José del Cabo art district provides boutique character. Tourist Corridor commands highest purchase prices but often delivers lowest net yields due to branded resort fees and restrictions. East Cape and Pacific Coast are emerging with thinner services.

## Sample unit economics: Cabo San Lucas marina 1BR

A well-located Cabo San Lucas marina 1BR purchased at USD 425,000 with USD 35,000 closing costs (all-in USD 460,000) can generate around USD 52,000 gross annually at 65% occupancy and USD 220 ADR during peak winter months. After management, cleaning, substantial HOA, trust fees, and luxury property taxes, realistic net yield settles around 4.8% — but conservative occupancy assumptions drop net toward 3.8%.

Assume $425K purchase, $35K closing (all-in $460K):

| Line | Annual USD |
|---|---:|
| Gross rent (65% occ, $220 ADR) | ~$52,000 |
| Management 27% | −$14,000 |
| Cleaning premium | −$2,800 |
| HOA $750/mo | −$9,000 |
| Trust + luxury taxes | −$2,400 |
| **NOI** | **~$23,800** |
| **Net yield** | **~5.2%** on all-in — optimistic |

Conservative 58% occupancy and $195 ADR drops net toward **3.8–4.2%** — realistic Cabo marina band.

## 2026 market recalibration

Los Cabos entered 2026 in luxury recalibration phase: properties that peaked at USD 800K–1.2M in 2022–2023 show extended marketing periods with motivated sellers accepting 10–20% haircuts. Entry-level condos (USD 350K–500K) hold pricing better but negotiation room exists. New branded phases still command premium but resale inventory offers value for patient buyers who understand luxury operational costs.

Post-2022 luxury peak signals:

- Properties over $800K showing **extended DOM**
- Motivated sellers on Tourist Corridor inventory
- Entry condos ($350K–500K) hold firmer
- Branded new phases maintain premium pricing

Negotiate on luxury resale — avoid paying 2023 peak pricing without compelling location advantage.

## Marina vs beach access strategy

Los Cabos offers two distinct positioning strategies: marina lifestyle condos with walkable access to restaurants, bars, and charter services, or beach-access properties with direct sand and surf appeal. Marina condos typically command higher year-round occupancy but at lower ADR; beachfront properties achieve premium rates but face seasonal concentration and hurricane exposure. Most first-time buyers should prioritize marina access for operational simplicity.

| Strategy | Pro | Con |
|---|---|---|
| Marina access | Year-round appeal, walkable | Lower ADR ceiling |
| Beach access | Premium ADR potential | Seasonal concentration |
| Tourist Corridor | Luxury amenities | Restrictions, fees |

Marina walkability beats beachfront isolation for STR management ease — especially remote ownership.

## Hurricane and seasonality impact

Los Cabos faces Pacific hurricane season from June through November, with September–October peak risk. Properties require comprehensive insurance and seasonal vacancy budgeting. Peak revenue concentrates November through April driven by US winter escape demand. Summer months (June–August) show lower occupancy even before hurricane considerations. Budget conservatively for shoulder season performance.

| Season | Los Cabos STR pattern |
|---|---|
| Nov–Apr | **Peak season** — highest ADR |
| May | Transition, still strong |
| Jun–Aug | Summer low, heat factor |
| Sep–Oct | **Hurricane season low** |

Underwrite full-year including Sep–Oct at 40–50% occupancy — not peak-only projections.

## Luxury HOA and operational reality

Los Cabos luxury condos carry substantially higher operational costs than Riviera Maya equivalents. HOA fees of USD 400–1,200 per month are standard for amenity-rich buildings, with some resort residentials exceeding USD 1,500 monthly. These fees cover concierge, pool/spa maintenance, landscaping, security, and often utilities — but compress net yields significantly. Factor true operational costs before calculating investment returns.

| Building type | Typical HOA monthly |
|---|---:|
| Standard luxury condo | $400–800 |
| Full-service marina | $600–1,200 |
| Resort residential | $1,000–1,500+ |
| Ultra-luxury branded | $1,500+ |

Request 24-month HOA statements before offer — special assessments common in luxury segment.

## Closing and legal structure

Los Cabos sits entirely within Mexico's restricted zone, so all foreign buyers hold title through fideicomiso bank trust. Budget 7–12% of purchase price for total closing costs including higher-value ISAI transfer tax, notario fees on luxury transactions, fideicomiso setup (USD 2,500–4,000), and independent legal review. Closing typically takes 45–90 days for luxury resale, longer for branded residential with additional documentation.

## Branded resort residential considerations

Los Cabos features extensive branded resort residential — Four Seasons, Montage, Zadun Ritz-Carlton Reserve, and others. These properties offer ultimate luxury amenities but come with significant operational restrictions: mandatory rental management contracts, resort fee structures, limited personal use windows, and HOA fees that can exceed USD 2,000 monthly. Investment returns typically fall below 3% net, making these lifestyle purchases rather than yield-focused investments.

| Brand consideration | Investment impact |
|---|---|
| Mandatory management | Eliminates operator choice |
| Resort fees | Additional revenue drag |
| Usage restrictions | Limited personal use |
| Ultra-luxury HOA | $1,500–2,500/month+ |

Branded residential = lifestyle luxury, not yield investment.

## Water and infrastructure awareness

Los Cabos development faces water supply constraints during construction booms. Established areas like Cabo San Lucas marina and central San José maintain reliable utilities, but fringe luxury developments may face intermittent service or require private systems. Verify water source and backup systems especially for East Cape or remote Tourist Corridor properties before finalizing luxury purchases.

Infrastructure checklist:
- Municipal water connection confirmed
- Backup water storage adequate
- Electrical service reliable (generator access)
- Internet/connectivity for STR management
- Road access maintained year-round

## Hurricane insurance and risk management

Los Cabos properties require comprehensive hurricane coverage — wind, flood, and business interruption for STR operations. Annual insurance costs USD 800–2,500+ depending on property value and deductibles. Some luxury buildings offer master policies but verify coverage limits. Budget 4–6 weeks annual closure for hurricane season impact on bookings.

Hurricane preparation:
- Comprehensive coverage verified annually
- STR calendar blocks during high-risk periods
- Property management includes storm prep services
- Guest communication protocols for weather events

## Resort residential rental programs

Many Los Cabos luxury developments offer rental programs through on-site management companies. These programs provide operational convenience but typically feature revenue splits of 50–70% to management (vs 25–30% for independent operators), mandatory participation requirements, and restricted personal use scheduling. Evaluate rental program terms carefully — they often reduce net yields below independent management alternatives.

| Program type | Revenue split | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| Resort mandatory | 50–70% to resort | Turnkey operations | Low net yield |
| Optional hotel pool | 60–65% to resort | Premium positioning | Revenue drag |
| Independent mgmt | 25–30% to mgmt | **Higher net yield** | More involvement |

Independent management typically delivers higher net — if quality operators available.

Los Cabos rewards buyers who prioritize **lifestyle-investment blend** over pure yield maximization. Marina access with walkable amenities, established luxury management, and realistic 3.8–5% net expectations can work for West Coast buyers wanting premium Mexico exposure. ## Pre-construction vs luxury resale

Los Cabos luxury pre-construction offers modern amenities and potential launch pricing but carries developer, delivery, and market timing risks. Luxury resale provides known operational history and immediate cash flow potential but commands premium pricing for premium locations. Given Los Cabos' luxury focus and seasonal market dynamics, experienced buyers often prefer resale with established STR performance over pre-construction uncertainty.

| Path | Pro | Con |
|---|---|---|
| Luxury resale | Known performance history | Premium pricing |
| Pre-construction | Modern design, potential discount | Developer/delivery risk |
| Resort residential new | Ultimate amenities | Highest cost, lowest yield |

Resale with 24-month STR history beats luxury pre-construction for first Los Cabos investment.
