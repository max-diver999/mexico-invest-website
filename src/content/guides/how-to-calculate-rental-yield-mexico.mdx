faq:
  - question: "How do you calculate gross rental yield in Mexico?"
    answer: "Gross yield = annual gross rental income ÷ purchase price (or all-in cost for precision). Example: $21,000 annual rent on $300,000 price = 7% gross. Marketing usually stops here."
  - question: "How do you calculate net rental yield?"
    answer: "Net yield = (gross rent − management − HOA − taxes − insurance − vacancy reserve − trust fees) ÷ all-in purchase cost. The same unit may net 4% or less after honest expenses."
  - question: "Should I use purchase price or all-in cost?"
    answer: "All-in cost, price plus 5–10% closing. Using price-only overstates yield by 30–50 basis points and distorts exit ISR basis planning."
  - question: "What occupancy rate should I assume?"
    answer: "65–70% annual average is prudent for established Playa Centro STR without building-specific history. 80%+ requires proof. 85% marketing assumptions are optimism."
  - question: "What management fee should I model?"
    answer: "25–30% of gross for full-service STR in Riviera Maya unless written quote says otherwise. 15% placeholders inflate net yield fictionally."
  - question: "How does HOA affect yield?"
    answer: "HOA is fixed annual drag, $200/month vs $500/month on same gross rent can swing net yield by 150+ basis points on $300K asset."
  - question: "How do I calculate cash-on-cash return?"
    answer: "Cash-on-cash = annual cash flow after debt service ÷ total cash invested (down payment + closing). Differs from unlevered net yield, model both if financing."
  - question: "What yield is realistic in Playa del Carmen 2026?"
    answer: "Net 4–5% on well-run Centro 1BR after 25% management and moderate HOA is realistic base case. Below 3% or above 6% net requires verification, not enthusiasm."
