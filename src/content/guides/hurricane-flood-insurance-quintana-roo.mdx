## What hurricane insurance covers in Quintana Roo (and what it skips)

Mexican property policies (polizas de danos) typically separate perils:

| Peril | Often included? | Owner action |
|---|---|---|
| Hurricane wind | Frequently yes | Confirm named-storm deductible |
| Flood / inundacion | Often excluded | Buy endorsement |
| Storm surge | Often excluded | Same endorsement bucket |
| Earth movement / sinkhole | Rare | Karst-specific review |
| Business interruption (STR) | Optional | Separate rider |

A policy that says "cubre huracanes" may still exclude water entering from ground level after heavy rain. That gap destroyed budgets in 2022–2025 rainy seasons across Solidaridad interior colonias.

---

## HOA master policy vs unit owner HO-6 equivalent

Condominium regimes carry a master policy on structure and common elements. Unit owners carry interior and contents coverage, the Mexico analog to US HO-6.

| Layer | Typical coverage | Flood gap |
|---|---|---|
| HOA master | Structure, roof, elevators, lobby | May exclude flood repairs → special assessment |
| Owner policy | Interior, contents, liability | Must add inundacion endorsement |
| STR rider | Business personal property | Confirm guest damage sublimits |

Request three documents at due diligence: HOA insurance declarations (last 12 months), any special assessment history from storm events, and a sample unit owner quote from a CNSF-licensed broker.

## How to buy flood coverage as a foreign owner

1. Engage CNSF-licensed broker: not only the seller's contact.
2. Inspect property in July–September if possible: observe drainage.
3. Request bilingual quote with peril list explicitly showing inundacion.
4. Match insured value to replacement cost, not purchase price nostalgia.
5. Coordinate with fideicomiso bank: some require proof of insurance annually.
6. Store policy PDF in exit folder for resale diligence.

Brokers familiar with expat owners often use GNP, AXA, or international Lloyd's programs for harder risks. FONDEN government disaster aid is not a substitute for private flood coverage.

## Wind deductibles vs flood deductibles (read both)

Hurricane policies may apply percentage deductibles only when a named storm enters a defined radius. Flood events from unnamed tropical waves may trigger a separate flat deductible or be excluded entirely.

| Event type | Typical trigger | Deductible style |
|---|---|---|
| Named hurricane wind | Storm within 100 km | 2–5% of insured value |
| Torrential rain flood | No named storm | Separate endorsement |
| Storm surge | Coastal exposure | Often excluded without rider |
| HOA common area | Master policy | Special assessment risk |

Ask your broker: "If Playa receives 180 mm rain in 24 hours without a named hurricane, am I covered?"

## Filing a claim after a flood or hurricane event

1. Document immediately: photos, video, water line marks, date/time.
2. Notify insurer within policy window: often 24–72 hours.
3. Mitigate damage: pump water, dry walls; keep receipts.
4. Notify HOA: common area damage may parallel your unit claim.
5. Do not begin major rebuild until adjuster visits unless emergency.
6. Translate adjuster report: understand excluded vs covered lines.
7. Track special assessments if HOA master policy gap exists.

Claims denied for "gradual seepage" are common on ground-floor units, pre-season documentation helps dispute weak denials.

## How to read a Mexican policy declarations page

Most foreign owners rely on a broker or property manager to translate Spanish policy documents. Before signing, verify these fields on the carátula (declarations page):

| Field (Spanish) | What to confirm | Red flag |
|---|---|---|
| Riesgos cubiertos | Named hurricane, wind, hail listed | Generic "all risk" without peril list |
| Inundación / fenómenos hidrometeorológicos | Explicitly included or excluded | Absent line item = usually excluded |
| Suma asegurada | Matches replacement cost, not purchase price | Under-insured by 30%+ |
| Deducible | Percent vs flat MXN amount | 2% of sum insured on $400k = $8k out-of-pocket |
| Coaseguro | Co-insurance penalty if under-insured | Hidden 20% owner share on claims |
| Exclusiones | Wear, gradual seepage, unoccupied 60+ days | STR vacancy clauses |

Ask the broker for a side-by-side English summary, not a verbal promise. Keep PDFs in cloud storage accessible to your US CPA and fideicomiso bank if they request proof annually.

STR owners should confirm whether business use or short-term rental voids residential policy language. Some carriers accept STR with disclosure; others require commercial endorsement at higher premium.

If HOA carries master policy, request certificate of insurance naming your unit or confirming walls-in coverage. Gap between master and unit policy is where post-hurricane disputes land in Solidaridad condominiums.

## Claim documentation requirements and adjuster workflow

Mexican hurricane and flood claims require immediate documentation discipline. Adjusters favor owners with pre-storm baseline photos and organized post-event evidence over verbal claims without time-stamped proof.

**48-hour claim documentation protocol:**

| Hour | Action | Why it matters | Tools needed |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0-6 | Photo/video water level marks, date/time stamp | Establishes severity before cleanup | Smartphone, email to yourself for timestamp |
| 6-12 | Pump water if safe, keep damaged items visible | Mitigation obligation, but preserve evidence | Wet-dry vac, don't discard furniture yet |
| 12-24 | Notify insurer via formal channel (email + phone) | Trigger claim window, some policies require 24h notice | Policy number, unit address, brief narrative |
| 24-48 | Document all damaged items with serial numbers | Contents claim detail | Receipts if available, photos of labels |
| 48-72 | Obtain HOA administrator letter on common damage | Parallel claim for special assessment defense | Formal request to administrator in Spanish |

**What kills claims faster than exclusions:**
- "We cleaned up before photos" — adjuster suspects minor damage exaggerated
- "I can't find my policy number" — delays by 7-14 days while broker searches
- No pre-storm condition photos — adjuster assumes prior wear
- Rebuilt drywall before adjuster visit — insurer denies "inability to inspect"

Smart owners maintain **cloud folder** (Google Photos, iCloud) with annual pre-hurricane season photos: HVAC units, appliance serial plates, wall/ceiling condition, furniture layout. Cost: zero. Claim speed-up: 3-6 weeks faster settlement.

**Adjuster site visit tips:**
- Be present or authorize manager with clear instructions
- Do not verbally inflate damages; stick to documented items
- If adjuster speaks only Spanish, hire translator, do not guess
- Request copy of adjuster report before leaving
- Ask explicitly: "Is this covered or excluded and why?"

Cross-border complication: US owners often assume adjuster process matches State Farm familiarity. Mexican adjusters work for insurer, not neutral third party. Consider hiring public adjuster (ajustador público) if claim over $25,000 USD, fee typically 5-10% of settlement but accelerates complex disputes.

## HOA special assessment history review (critical for ground-floor buyers)

Condominiums with master policies excluding flood or carrying high deductibles pass hurricane repair costs to unit owners via special assessments. One undisclosed assessment history can erase three years of rental yield.

**What to request from HOA administrator before offer:**

| Document | Lookback period | Red flag threshold |
|---|---|---|
| Special assessment history | 5 years minimum | 2+ flood assessments = chronic drainage issue |
| Master policy declarations page | Current + prior 2 renewals | Flood exclusion or deductible over 5% of building value |
| Reserve fund balance | Current | Under 20% of annual budget = assessment likely next storm |
| Pending or threatened assessments | Open items | Any flood-related item in assembly minutes |

**Typical special assessment triggers post-storm:**

| Building damage | Master policy status | Owner assessment range per unit (indicative) |
|---|---|---|
| Lobby flooding, elevator damage | Covered with $50K deductible | $2,000–$5,000 per unit in 40-unit building |
| Parking level surge, vehicles damaged | Flood excluded | $8,000–$15,000 per unit |
| Roof membrane failure, multiple units | Wind covered, but age exclusion | $3,000–$10,000 per unit |
| Structural column saltwater exposure | Covered but betterment clause | $5,000–$12,000 per unit for upgrade |

Ground-floor units in buildings with weak master policies face **double hit**: own unit flood damage (if no endorsement) plus pro-rata share of common area assessment.

**Negotiation leverage:** Sellers in buildings with 2+ prior flood assessments should discount 5-8% or provide escrow hold-back for next assessment. Buyers accepting full price on assessment-prone buildings overpay relative to clean-history comparables.

Request assembly minutes in Spanish with translation, search for: "cuota extraordinaria," "evaluación especial," "inundación," "huracán," "fondo de reserva insuficiente."

## Carrier market evolution post-2024 Atlantic season

2024 Atlantic hurricane season plus 2022-2023 intense rainfall events shifted Quintana Roo carrier appetite. Some international programs exited market, others raised premiums 30-50%, and deductibles increased.

**Market segments 2026:**

| Carrier type | Appetite for Quintana Roo flood | Premium trend | Policy language |
|---|---|---|---|
| Domestic Mexican CNSF carriers (GNP, AXA) | Selective, ground-floor harder | Stable to +15% YoY | Spanish policy with peril exclusions |
| Lloyd's syndicates via brokers | Available for quality buildings | +25-40% vs 2021 | English-friendly but expensive |
| US surplus lines | Rare, non-admitted in Mexico | Limited availability | May not satisfy fideicomiso bank |
| Parametric / index programs | Emerging, experimental | Flat premium, payout based on storm metrics not damage | Complex for individual owners |

**What changed for foreign buyers:**
- Pre-2022: Flood endorsement often $300-600 annual, minimal underwriting
- 2024-2026: Flood $600-1,500 annual, elevation survey, drainage inspection, prior claim history mandatory

Buildings completed 2015-2025 with no claim history get better rates than older stock with multiple events. New construction in 2026 may qualify for discounts if elevated design and modern drainage systems documented.

**Broker shopping strategy:**
- Obtain 3 quotes from independent CNSF brokers
- Request English peril summary side-by-side with Spanish declarations
- Compare deductible structures: flat MXN vs percentage of sum insured
- Verify carrier rating (AM Best B+ minimum for foreign buyers comfort)
- Confirm broker holds active CNSF license (search registry online)

Avoid captive agents from developer closing table who represent one carrier only. Independent brokers access multiple markets and negotiate harder.

Cross-reference Pacific dynamics: [Hurricane Insurance BCS](/guides/hurricane-insurance-bcs/) — Los Cabos market faced similar hardening post-Otis 2023, but Atlantic Quintana Roo has deeper carrier history, BCS is thinner market with fewer options.

## Seasonal premium adjustments and payment timing

Some Quintana Roo policies allow monthly payment plans, but annual lump sum at policy inception (typically May-June before hurricane season) often secures 5-10% discount and avoids mid-season cancellation if payment missed.

**Payment timing optimization:**

| Payment structure | Total annual cost | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|---|
| Annual lump May | $1,200 baseline | Discount, no missed payment risk | Large cash outflow |
| Semi-annual (May + Nov) | $1,260 (+5%) | Spread cost | Risk of November non-renewal if May claim filed |
| Monthly auto-debit | $1,320 (+10%) | Cash flow friendly | Cancel risk if card expires, foreign transaction fees |
| Quarterly | $1,290 (+7.5%) | Balance | Administrative burden |

STR owners should align insurance payment with **highest revenue quarter** (December-February) to avoid dipping into peak-season gross for June insurance lump sum. Budget insurance in pro forma as separate line, not buried in "miscellaneous."

**Mid-season policy changes (dangerous):** Switching carriers in August after rate shopping may leave gap if old policy cancels before new binds. Maintain continuous coverage, even if overpaying, rather than risk 3-week gap during peak storm window.

Fideicomiso banks increasingly request proof of insurance at annual trust fee payment. Missing proof can trigger bank requirement letters and delays. Store insurance PDF in same folder as trust docs, update annually.

Compare tax treatment: Hurricane insurance premiums are generally deductible against rental income on Mexican RFC filings and US Schedule E. Coordinate with contador and US CPA to maximize deductions. Flood endorsement specifically may qualify for enhanced deductions in high-risk zones depending on current tax code.
