  - question: "Can first-time buyers close remotely?"
    answer: "Yes via power of attorney and documented wires to notario or escrow, common for US and Canadian buyers. Remote is not a reason to skip independent counsel or HOA bylaws review."
  - question: "Is fideicomiso scary for first-time buyers?"
    answer: "It is standard for coastal Mexico, not a workaround. Banks hold title in trust; you retain use, rent, sell, and inherit rights. Confusion about trusts causes less loss than ejido purchases or skipped legal review."
  - question: "Should first-timers buy pre-construction?"
    answer: "Usually no. Resale with known HOA history and immediate STR path reduces delivery and permit risk. Pre-construction suits experienced buyers who verify developer escrow and track record."
  - question: "What is the biggest first-purchase mistake?"
    answer: "Chasing the lowest sticker in oversupplied towers, especially Tulum Region 15, without building-level due diligence. Cheap purchase price often pairs with thin resale and net yields under 3%."
  - question: "How long does a first resale closing take?"
    answer: "Expect 30–90 days from accepted offer to notarized escritura. Fideicomiso establishment at a new bank adds weeks. Pre-construction follows developer milestones over 18–36 months, different risk profile."
  - question: "Do first-time buyers need a Mexican corporation?"
    answer: "Rarely for one vacation condo. Default fideicomiso unless an accountant documents multi-asset business need. Corporations add compliance without simplifying STR for most individuals."
