  - question: "What are the steps to buy property in Mexico?"

Resale typically takes 30–90 days from accepted offer."
  - question: "How long does it take to buy a condo in Mexico?"
    answer: "Resale closings commonly run 30–90 days. New fideicomiso adds 2–4 weeks. Pre-construction follows developer milestone schedules over 18–36 months. Cash buyers with clean title close faster than financed or messy-chain transactions."
  - question: "Do I need to be in Mexico to close?"
    answer: "Not always. Power of attorney (poder notarial) allows a representative to sign at notario. You still need verified wire paths, escrow discipline, and independent legal review, remote closing is common but not a reason to skip DD."
  - question: "When do I pay the deposit?"
    answer: "After offer acceptance, typically 5–10% into escrow or notario-controlled account, not directly to seller's personal account. Never wire large sums without documented escrow structure."
  - question: "Who chooses the notario?"
    answer: "Traditionally buyer proposes; parties can negotiate. Notario must be licensed in the state of the property. Your attorney should review fee quote before commitment."
  - question: "What happens on closing day?"
    answer: "Buyer (or POA) signs escritura before notario, pays remaining balance and closing costs, ISAI is calculated, trust beneficiary rights are recorded, keys release per contract. Registry inscription follows within days to weeks."
  - question: "What should I do the week after closing?"
    answer: "Confirm registry inscription, obtain CFDI invoices documenting basis for future ISR, set up utilities and HOA accounts, engage property manager if STR intent, file Mexican tax registrations if renting."
  - question: "What is the biggest mistake in the buying process?"
    answer: "Skipping independent title review and wiring deposit to seller before clean DD. Secondary mistake: using seller's attorney as your only counsel."
