Dominio pleno conversion, transitioning ejido parcels to private property, exists in law but:

- Requires assembly votes (typically a two-thirds supermajority)

- Costs roughly USD 15,000–50,000 in cumulative legal, survey, and administrative fees
- Can fail mid-process with sunk costs unrecoverable
- Is not a turnkey product for foreign cash buyers
