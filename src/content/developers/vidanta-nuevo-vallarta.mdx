## ⚠️ Membership model disclaimer (read first)

Vidanta sells product that may not equate to classic Mexican condo ownership. Many offerings operate as vacation club memberships, fractional interests, or program-based usage rights, structurally different from buying a fideicomiso-held condo with full sell/rent/inherit rights under standard Riviera Nayarit practice.

Before paying any deposit:

1. Hire an independent attorney: not sales-floor counsel.
2. Obtain full contract translation to English if you are a US buyer.
3. Confirm whether you receive beneficial rights in a trust, membership points, or timeshare-like obligations.
4. Compare against fee-simple or fideicomiso condo alternatives in [Nuevo Vallarta](/areas/nuevo-vallarta/) and [Puerto Vallarta](/areas/puerto-vallarta/).
5. Understand exit and resale restrictions: club inventory often lacks MLS liquidity.

Mexico Invest publishes this profile for education and search intent, we do not endorse membership products as equivalent to standard investment condos. Verify all claims with licensed counsel.

## Product types: what you might actually be buying

Vidanta marketing uses residence, luxury suite, club, and investment language interchangeably. Underwriting requires separating:

| Product class | Typical rights | Investor treatment |
|---|---|---|
| Fee-simple / fideicomiso condo | Use, rent, sell, inherit | Standard DD |
| Fractional / club points | Usage weeks, exchange | Liquidity risk |

## Financial mechanics: fees that compress net yield

Resort-scale HOAs fund pools, golf, security, and landscaping, predictable $400–700/month on many Nayarit stacks, higher in luxury tiers.

Add program fees, exchange charges, special assessments, and rental management splits common in club structures.

| Cost line | Typical range | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| HOA monthly | $400–700+ | Resort amenities |
| STR management | 20–35% gross | If independent STR allowed |
| Fideicomiso annual | $500–800 | If trust structure used |
| Closing stack | 5–10% | ISAI Nayarit 2–3% |
| Program fees | Variable | Club-specific |

Insider tip: Request 5-year HOA history and special assessment log, resort infrastructure is capex-heavy.

## Legal and ownership path

Coastal Nayarit falls in Mexico's restricted zone, foreigners typically use fideicomiso for true real estate interests. Membership contracts may not use fideicomiso, they may be personal property rights or contractual bundles.

| Check | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Escritura vs membership agreement | Defines resale |
| SRE permit if trust | Foreign coastal compliance |
| Ejido exclusion | National red flag #1 |
| CFDI on purchase | Future ISR cost basis |
| Notario involvement | True deed vs club receipt |

## Risks specific to Vidanta and club models

1. Resale liquidity: club points often trade at discounts vs original pitch.
2. Fee escalation: maintenance rises with resort aging.
3. Usage vs investment conflict: personal weeks reduce rentable calendar.
4. STR restrictions: HOA or program may block independent Airbnb.
5. Sales pressure: off-plan incentives mask contract weaknesses.
6. Tax complexity: US Schedule E may not map cleanly to club income, CPA required.

## Due diligence checklist (non-negotiable)

1. Independent bilingual attorney: translate entire contract, not summary sheet.
2. Classify product: deed, trust, membership, fractional, write it down.
3. Compare fee-simple alternatives: at least two non-club comps.
4. Model net yield with all program fees: [Gross vs Net](/guides/gross-vs-net-yield-mexico/).
5. Resale clause: who can you sell to and at what penalty?
6. STR legality: HOA + Bahía de Banderas municipal rules.
7. Tax planning: [US Taxes Mexico Rental](/guides/us-taxes-mexico-rental-property/) + Mexican accountant.
8. Never wire deposit before counsel signs off.
